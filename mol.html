Assignment 01  ·  Baton Rouge, Louisiana

Mall of Louisiana

Chick-fil-A Mall of Louisiana

Interim Manager  ·  April 17, 2025 – October 11, 2025  ·  6 Months

Assignment Lead  ·  Walt Anderson

100% Quality Improvement Visit Score
$10,600 Record-Setting Catering Delivery
8.17% Net Profit Margin — July 2025
$79 Labor Productivity Per Hour
3 Remarkable Futures Scholarship Winners
Context

The Situation

The Mall of Louisiana was not a clean hand-off. There was no accountability infrastructure in place. The team was facing severe absenteeism. Equipment failures were ongoing. Pest issues had gone unresolved. The previous culture had no framework for hospitality, no recognition systems, and no clear development path for the leaders who had stayed.

What started as an assignment that was supposed to be short became something longer — and eventually, I was the only Interim Manager on the grounds. I had to make a choice about what I was going to build with what was in front of me.

Results

What We Built

By Q2, the restaurant earned a perfect 100% on its Quality Improvement Visit — the highest score achievable. That held at 98% in Q3, demonstrating not just peak performance but consistency. In July 2025, the location posted an 8.17% net profit margin, the best the store had seen in years, alongside labor productivity of $79 per labor hour. The restaurant ranked in the top 20% of mall CEM scores across nearly every category.

The largest catering delivery the location had ever executed — $10,600 — was completed on time and with excellence. Three leaders from the team were nominated for and won Chick-fil-A Remarkable Futures Scholarships. Watching those young people receive recognition for their potential, many of whom had never been told they were capable of something like that, is a memory I carry as much as any business result.

If it were up to me, I would hand you the keys to a restaurant and make you an operator. I don't say that often, and I don't say it lightly.

Tracy Roper, Team Director — after touring the restaurant and reviewing operations

Stories

Moments That Defined It

The GOAT Table System

There was no framework for hospitality when I arrived — no structure for how to interact with guests at the table. I built the GOAT system from scratch: Greet, Offer, Ask, Thank. It was a hospitality framework built around bringing intentional Second Mile Service to a team that had never been trained on it. Watching team members who had never thought about table touches start executing it with genuine care was one of the most rewarding things I saw during this assignment.

The $10,600 Catering Delivery

The largest catering delivery the location had ever attempted. We planned it, we staffed it, and we executed it on time and with excellence. It became a proof point for what the team was capable of — and a moment of genuine pride in an assignment that asked a lot of everyone on the floor.

The Remarkable Futures Nominations

I nominated three leaders from this team for the Chick-fil-A Remarkable Futures Scholarship. All three won. Several of them had never been recognized for their potential before. Watching them receive that recognition — knowing what it meant to them — is a memory that sits above the business results for me. This is why the work matters.
