Context

The Situation

I arrived at Altamesa & McCart on October 12, 2025, as the first Interim Manager on the grounds during a confidential operator transition — one of the most complex assignment types in the program. The restaurant had been run into the ground. EcoSure food safety scores had deteriorated to a 5 under the previous operator's final months. The business was losing nearly 12% net profit. Facilities were failing. The community had been underserved for years.

In the months that followed, the team navigated power outages, a leaking roof, sewage backups, a broken water heater, and the emotional weight of working alongside an operator being let go — all while rebuilding culture, developing leaders, and managing over $100,000 in facilities repairs.

Results

What We Built

The QIV score rose from 76.6% to 91.1% — a 14.5-point improvement in a single quarter. The EcoSure food safety score, which had deteriorated to a 5, improved to a 4 by Q4 2025 and reached a 3 in the first official scored visit of 2026. In December, just 30 days after the restaurant was losing nearly 12% net profit, we turned it to over 9% positive. By January 2026, sales were up 9.98% and transactions were up 13.70% year over year.

In the final weeks of the assignment, the restaurant was selected to host an international LDP readiness class, welcoming four participants from Canada and one from Puerto Rico for in-field training. A restaurant that had been failing operationally just months before was now being used as a model environment for brand-new LDP participants to learn in. Shortly after, the restaurant was also scheduled to host operator fundamentals. What was built did not need me there to sustain it.

Isaac is on track and performing ahead of expectations for this point in the program. His relational leadership has been transformational for a team that experienced years of instability.

Walt Anderson, LDP Transition Lead — Written Assessment

Stories

Moments That Defined It

The Chess King

The night I found a wooden king chess piece lying in the parking lot — in perfect condition — I held it for a long time. The king in chess moves slowly, but it controls the fate of the entire game. Truett Cathy built this entire brand on that same conviction: he never called himself an owner. He called himself a steward, because he believed everything entrusted to him ultimately belonged to God. Standing in that parking lot, holding that small wooden king, something settled in me. God controls the game and the outcome. My job is simply to be faithful with what is in front of me.

It Feels Like Chick-fil-A Again

Near the beginning of the transition, a woman stopped me in the dining room. She told me there was a local Facebook group that had been following the transition closely. She had not been to the restaurant in years because of what it had become, and she thanked me for what she was seeing. That same week, one of our FOH leaders told me it finally felt like he was working at a Chick-fil-A again. These were the moments that kept the team going through the hardest challenges.

The International Class

In the final weeks of the assignment, our restaurant was selected to host an international LDP readiness class — four participants from Canada, one from Puerto Rico, here for in-field training. A restaurant that had been failing operationally just months before was now the model environment for brand-new LDP participants to learn in. Shortly after, operator fundamentals were also scheduled there. What we built did not need me there to sustain it. That alone is the measure of the work.
